# Understanding Modern Immunology

Our immune system is a complex network of cells, tissues, and organs that work together to defend the body against attacks by foreign invaders. These are primarily microbes—tiny, infectious organisms such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungi.

*Figure 1: Overview of the human immune system's primary components.*

## The First Line of Defense

The immune system is amazingly complex. It can recognize and remember millions of different enemies, and it can produce secretions and cells to match and destroy nearly all of them.

### Adaptive Immunity

Unlike innate immunity, adaptive immunity is specific to the pathogen presented. The adaptive immune response is meant to attack non-self pathogens but can sometimes make mistakes and attack itself.
